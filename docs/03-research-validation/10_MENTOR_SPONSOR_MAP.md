# /10_MENTOR_SPONSOR_MAP.md

## Recommended primary track
**Future of Work**

This idea is most clearly a Future of Work project because the core product is an AI-assisted operating layer for a small Filipino customer support team: intake, classification, routing, human review, and contribution visibility. The strongest story is not “AI for support,” but **who benefits when service work is reshaped by AI**. That is exactly where the event’s Future of Work framing is strongest.

## Optional secondary track: keep or drop
**Keep, but subordinate it hard.**

Use **Collective Prosperity** only as a supporting lens, not as co-equal framing.

Recommended rule:
- **Slide 1 / first sentence / title:** Future of Work only
- **Impact / why it matters slide:** mention Collective Prosperity briefly
- **Q&A:** use it to defend why visibility and legibility matter for workers, not just managers

Do **not** pitch this as a cooperative ownership project. Right now the secondary-track case is only credible because:
- routing logic is legible
- low-confidence cases stay under human review
- contribution visibility gives workers more visibility into how work is distributed

That is meaningful, but it is still a light Collective Prosperity claim. If you overplay it, the story gets messy fast.

## Why this track framing works
The event’s Future of Work framing is unusually specific to the Philippines: services, outsourcing, overseas labor, and the question of **who benefits as AI changes work**. Your draft already matches that better than the other tracks.

Why it works:
- The user is a **5–20 person Filipino customer support team**.
- The pain is **manual triage, opaque routing, and delayed QA**.
- The product keeps AI in an **assistive** role rather than replacing judgment.
- The demo is a clean vertical slice, which helps on feasibility.
- The broader meaning is that small service teams can operate with more trust, consistency, and visibility without needing enterprise tooling.

Why the secondary lens is only partial:
- You are **not** redistributing ownership in a hard structural sense.
- You are **not** building a marketplace, profit-sharing system, or co-op governance engine.
- Your only real Collective Prosperity wedge is that the system makes work allocation more legible and less opaque.

That is enough for a supporting sentence, but not enough to lead the whole pitch.

## Exact questions to ask each person
### Juliana “Nana” Luz / Softype
1. Does this read to you as a real **Future of Work** improvement for small support teams, or does it still look like generic helpdesk tooling?
2. What would make this feel more like a move from **mechanical work** to **knowledge-based work**?
3. In a small BPO-like environment, would visible routing reasons and a low-confidence review queue actually increase trust, or just add noise?
4. Which one matters more in the pitch: faster triage, better quality control, or more legible work allocation?

### Bestsign team
1. What is the minimum explanation a user needs to trust an AI recommendation in an operational workflow?
2. Where should we draw the line between automation and human review so the system feels credible, not risky?
3. What is the best way to show “confidence” and escalation in a demo without overwhelming people?
4. What part of our flow most resembles the strongest pattern in your LegalAI example, and what part currently feels weak?

### YGG Pilipinas / Mon Villareal
1. Does “Contribution Visibility” sound like real worker value, or just a nicer label for management reporting?
2. If we claim this helps workers, what exact outcome should we point to: skill growth, fairer distribution, less random escalation, or clearer recognition?
3. What would make this feel like infrastructure that includes workers rather than infrastructure that simply measures them?
4. Is there a better phrase than “worker-led” for a system that improves team legibility without overclaiming ownership?

### Groupmuse / Kyle Schmolze
1. Are we stretching by using “worker-led” if the MVP only shows routing visibility and contribution visibility?
2. What is the most honest language for our current scope: team visibility, contribution visibility, shared operations, or something else?
3. If we wanted one small but credible ownership cue in MVP, what would it be?
4. What would make the Collective Prosperity framing feel grounded instead of decorative?
